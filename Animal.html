Dogs

The dog (Canis familiaris or Canis lupus familiaris)is a domesticated descendant of the wolf. Also called the domestic dog, it is derived from an ancient, extinct wolf, and the modern wolf is the dog's nearest living relative. Many work as assistance dogs, helping humans!

Their nose secretes a special mucus that helps to absorb these chemicals, and then they lick their noses to sample them, helping them understand what the smell is.
Dogs are thought to be as smart as the wolves(and much easier to train!), so many owners teach them commands and tricks.

1. Dogs are the most popular pet on the planet!
2. They can learn over 100 words and gestures!
3. Dog noses are at least 40x more sensitive than ours!



Cats

The cat (Felis catus) isa domestic species of small carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae and is commonly referred to as the domestic cat or house cat to distinguish it from the wild members of the family.

A cat has no collarbone, so it can fit through any opening the size of its head. The squishiest of cat facts!
According to Veterinary Hub, Cats actually spend 70% of their lives sleeping, which works out to around 13-16 hours a day. It’s a cat’s life!

1. The hearing of the average cat is at least five times keener than that of a human adult.
2. In the largest cat breed, the average male weighs approximately 20 pounds.
3. Domestic cats spend about 70 percent of the day sleeping. And 15 percent of the day grooming.
